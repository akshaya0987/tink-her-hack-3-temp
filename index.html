Top Destinations

vagamon

Surrounded by lush tea gardens, verdant pine forests and small waterfalls, Vagamon is home to three hills, namely, Thangal hill, Murugan hill and Kurisumala. The hill station is perfect for adventure-seekers and nature-lovers.Vagamon's culture is a rich blend of traditions and modernity, deeply rooted in Malayali customs. The local festivals, especially Onam and Vishu, are celebrated with great fervor, showcasing vibrant performances, traditional games, and sumptuous feasts.

Chokramudi peak in Munnar

Surrender yourself to the unspoiled beauty of Chokramudi Peak on your Munnar tour. An ideal destination for nature buffs and adventure seekers, Chokramudi Peak is an isolated peak in this hill station that promises to offer a kind of experience you’ll cherish for your entire life. It’s a beautiful location encompassed by snow-capped ranges from all around the area and has scenic surroundings for tourists looking to attain peace of mind in tranquility. Located in the Eravikulam National Park and at a height of nearly 7200 feet above sea level, Chokramudi Peak is one of the highest peaks in Munnar that welcomes people from different corners of the world to look over the Idukki Dam, lush-green tea plantations, dense forestation, and jaw-dropping mountains from the top.

Nelliyampathy

Nelliyampathy is a hill station within the Nelliyampathy Forest Reserve, in the southwest Indian state of Kerala. The road to it, which goes through the dense forests of the Western Ghats, is known for its many hairpin turns. To the west is Kesavan Para, a vantage point with views of nearby tea plantations. Farther west is the 19th-century Pothundi Dam. Northeast is the Seetharkundu Viewpoint, with waterfalls nearby

Edakkal Caves

The Edakkal caves are two natural caves at a remote location at Edakkal, 25 km from Kalpetta in the Wayanad district of Kerala in India. They lie 1,200 m above sea level on Ambukutty Mala, near an ancient trade route connecting the high mountains of Mysore to the ports of the Malabar Coast.
